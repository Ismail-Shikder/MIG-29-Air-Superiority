Dominating the
Dogfight Since 1977

Developed by Mikoyan Design Bureau, the MiG-29 first flew in 1977 and entered service in 1983. Known for extreme agility and twin RD-33 engines, it remains active in over 25 nations worldwide.

Twin RD-33 Engines

Two Klimov RD-33 turbofans provide a 1:1 thrust-to-weight ratio, enabling vertical climbs and Mach 2.25 speeds. Unique dorsal louvers allow the jet to breathe while the main intakes stay shut to protect against debris on rough runways.

Weaponry & Payload

The MiG-29 features six hardpoints for a 4,000 kg payload, including R-73 'Archer' and R-27 missiles. Its 30mm GSh-30-1 cannon and helmet-mounted aiming system make it a lethal dogfighter, now upgraded for laser-guided ground strikes.

Global Service

In service since 1983, the "Fulcrum" is legendary for its super-maneuverability. Proven in conflicts from the Gulf War to Ukraine, it remains the backbone of 25+ nations, including India, Poland, and Bangladesh.
